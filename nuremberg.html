Things to Know

Carry-on: Ryanair allows one small bag (40 × 20 × 25 cm) free. A 10-kg cabin bag needs Priority or extra fee.

Boarding: doors close 20 min before departure. Late = denied — Ryanair is strict.

Online check-in opens 24 h before (2 h with paid Priority). Print or save the pass — paper or app.

i Aboard the 737

The Boeing 737-800 carries 189 passengers in a 3-3 layout. Ryanair's seats do not recline — the airline removed the mechanism years ago to save weight and turnaround time. Lavatories at the front and rear; tray tables fold up; window views to the right (over the Alps) are the prize on this northbound route.

Chapter the Second

FROM RUNWAY TO RAMPARTS

Ankunft
NUE airport · 7 km · 12 minutes by U-Bahn
Fig. 3 typical descent profile · cruise → touchdown

Albrecht Dürer Airport sits oddly close to the city — closer than almost any European capital airport to its centre. A €3.70 ticket and twelve minutes of U-Bahn deposit you at Hauptbahnhof, ready to walk into the medieval old town.

i U-Bahn 2 · the simplest way

Walk out of Terminal 1, follow signs for U-Bahn. The U2 line runs driverless — there is no front cab, so sit in the front seat for a children's-eye view of the tunnel rushing toward you.

Ride to Hauptbahnhof in 12 minutes. From there, the medieval gate of Königstor is across the square — the city walls begin five steps later.

Tickets · 2026

Single (Einzelfahrkarte): €3.70 · valid for 90 min, one direction.

Day pass (TagesTicket Solo): €9.30 · all VAG transport, all day.

Group day pass (TagesTicket Plus): €13.70 · up to 6 people.

Buy at the blue VAG machines (English available) or via the VAG Fahrplan & Tickets app.

no turnstiles — but check your ticket! random fare control

ii By Taxi

A taxi from NUE to the centre runs roughly €20–25 and 15 minutes. Convenient with luggage; rarely necessary otherwise. Uber is not common in Nuremberg — use FreeNow or just the airport rank.

Chapter the Third

DIE ALTSTADT

die Heimliche Hauptstadt
"the secret capital of the Holy Roman Empire"
Fig. 4 the rooftops of Nürnberg

Nürnberg's old town is a sandstone-red riddle of half-timbered houses, gabled roofs, and the Pegnitz running through the middle. Two-thirds was rebuilt after 1945 — done so carefully that even Nürnbergers cannot always tell new from old.

i The Heart
★
Hauptmarkt
der schöne Brunnen
MAP
The market square with the gilded Gothic Schöner Brunnen ("Beautiful Fountain", 1396) at one corner. Find the brass ring set in the iron grille — turn it three times for luck. The square also holds the daily market, and from late November the world's most famous Christkindlesmarkt.
at noon, watch the Männleinlaufen on the Frauenkirche — seven electors bow to the Emperor
ii Sandstone Skyline
  • St. Sebald MAP — the older of two great Gothic churches; Peter Vischer's bronze shrine within.
  • St. Lorenz MAP — south of the river; Veit Stoss's hanging Annunciation floats above the choir.
  • Heilig-Geist-Spital MAP — the medieval hospital straddling the Pegnitz; the picture-book bridge view of Nürnberg.
  • Weißgerbergasse MAP — the prettiest half-timbered street to survive the war intact.
  • Henkersteg MAP — the covered "Hangman's Bridge" with a small museum in the tower.

touch the brass ring on the fountain — three turns!

Chapter the Fourth

KAISERBURG

die Burg über der Stadt
imperial castle · 11th century · 351 m above sea

Every Holy Roman Emperor between 1050 and 1571 stayed here. The view from the Sinwellturm is the same view they saw — sandstone roofs falling away to the south, green Franconian hills to the north.

▲
Imperial Castle
Kaiserburg
MAP
A complex of three parts: the Kaiserburg (imperial), the Burggrafenburg (count's), and the city's own fortifications. Climb the Sinwellturm — a round tower with a 360° view — and descend to the Tiefer Brunnen, a 50-metre well that the guide will lower a candle into. The candle keeps shrinking long after you stop seeing it.
€7 entry · climb early before tour groups · ~1 hour suffices
i A Brief History

The first stones were laid around 1050 on a sandstone outcrop already used by the Salian emperors. Frederick Barbarossa expanded it; Charles IV codified its imperial role in the Goldene Bulle of 1356, which decreed that every newly elected Holy Roman Emperor must hold his first Reichstag in Nürnberg. Until 1571, every emperor came. The city was, briefly, the closest thing the Empire had to a capital.

Don't Miss

The double chapel — two stacked chapels, one above the other; the emperor sat in the upper, the household in the lower, joined by an opening in the floor.

The Sinwellturm — the round tower; 113 steps; on a clear day you can see all the way to Bamberg.

The Burggarten — the small terraced garden on the city-facing side; free and the best free view in Nürnberg.

free entry to the gardens — perfect for a sunset look

— enea silvio piccolomini, 1457

Chapter the Fifth

ALBRECHT DÜRER

der Meister der Linie
painter · printmaker · mathematician · son of Nürnberg

Born here in 1471, Dürer made Nürnberg the print capital of Europe. The man who drew a hare so finely you can count its eyelashes also drew the first proper rhinoceros most Europeans had ever seen — without ever seeing one.

i Dürer's House
A
Albrecht-Dürer-Haus
where the master worked
MAP
Half-timber, four storeys, just below the castle. Dürer lived and worked here from 1509 until his death in 1528. The kitchen is largely unchanged; the printmaking room has working replicas of his press; the top-floor garret holds copies of his greatest works.
€6 · audio guide narrated by "Frau Dürer" herself · skip the multimedia annex
ii Other Cultural Stops
  • Germanisches Nationalmuseum MAP — Germany's largest museum of German cultural history. The world's oldest globe (Behaim, 1492) lives here.
  • Documentation Center · Reichsparteitagsgelände MAP — a sober, essential confrontation with the Nazi rallies and their aftermath. Allow 2 hours.
  • Memorium Nuremberg Trials MAP — Courtroom 600, where Göring stood trial. Open most days.
  • Spielzeugmuseum MAP — the Toy Museum. Nürnberg has been Europe's toy capital since the 14th century.
  • Neues Museum MAP — modern art and design in a striking glass arc. Free Sunday afternoon.

Dürer's "Selbstbildnis" — copy in the house, original in Munich

A Detour

Walk five minutes west of Dürer's house to Tiergärtnertorplatz MAP — the small square with the medieval gate, the half-timber wall and a bronze hare (after Dürer's Feldhase). On warm evenings the city's young drink beer here on the cobbles. It is, quietly, the best square in Nürnberg.

Chapter the Sixth

DREI IM WECKLA

a Franconian table
bratwurst · lebkuchen · rotbier · Schäufele

Nürnberg is a small-sausage town in a big-beer country. Eat three little sausages on a roll standing in the street, then eat seven more on a pewter plate sitting down.

i Nürnberger Bratwürste

The Nürnberger Rostbratwurst is finger-sized and finger-shaped, made of pork seasoned with marjoram. They are protected by EU geographical indication — only sausages made within the city walls (historically) can carry the name.

The classic order is Drei im Weckla — "three in a roll" — three sausages in a crusty Brötchen with mustard. Eaten standing, in five minutes, at a window.

DREI IM WECKLA · €4.50 SECHS · €11 SAUERKRAUT · €3
⌘
Bratwurstherzla
since 1419
MAP
Off the Hauptmarkt, in a tiny crooked half-timber house. Six or eight sausages on pewter, with sauerkraut and a Rotbier. The dining room is the size of a stable. Cash preferred.
other classics: Bratwursthäusle by St. Sebald · Bratwurst-Röslein at the Markt
ii Lebkuchen — Year-Round

Lebkuchen are not just for Christmas in Nürnberg. The honeyed gingerbreads have been baked here since the 14th century, when monks crystallised the recipe. The best — labelled Elisenlebkuchen — must be at least 25% nuts and contain almost no flour.

Stop at Lebkuchen-Schmidt on the Hauptmarkt or Wicklein in Hauptbahnhof. They keep for months — they fly home well.

iii Rotbier & Schäufele
  • Rotbier — Nürnberg's reddish-amber regional beer, brewed since the Middle Ages and almost extinct by 1990. Try at Hausbrauerei Altstadthof.
  • Schäufele — roast pork shoulder, crackling skin scored in a diamond grid, served with potato dumpling (Kloß) and dark gravy. Sunday lunch food.
  • Schwarzbier — black lager. Not Bavarian Helles — this is Franconia, and the beer is darker and drier.
  • Drei in der Schale — the rare sit-down version: three little sausages in a tin bowl with horseradish.
  • Sauer'm Zipfel — sausages braised in vinegar, onion, and Rotbier. A winter dish.

say "ein Rotbier, bitte" — they will smile

Late April in Franconia is changeable. Sun in the morning, hail in the afternoon, sun again at six. Pack a thin rain shell and a warm layer. Beer-garden weather begins around now if the day is generous.

ii A Few Words
  • Servus · hello / goodbye (Bavarian/Austrian, used freely in Franconia too)
  • Grüß Gott · "greet God" — formal hello
  • Bitte · please / you're welcome / pardon
  • Danke schön · thank you (very much)
  • Eine Brezn, bitte · "a pretzel, please"
  • Zahlen, bitte · "the bill, please" (point at the table)
  • Prost! · cheers — look in the eyes when you clink

tip ~5–10% — round up at cafés, more at restaurants

iii Practical Trivia
Useful

Cash: still common — many small shops, bratwurst stands and bakeries are cash only. Carry €30–50.

Sundays: nearly all shops shut. Restaurants and museums open. Plan groceries on Saturday.

Tap water: safe and excellent. Restaurants don't usually serve it — order "Leitungswasser" if you must.

Quiet hours: Sundays and after 22:00 are properly observed. No drilling, no loud music.
